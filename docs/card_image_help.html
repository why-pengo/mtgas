Card Photography Guide

Taking great card photos with your iPhone 17 Pro Max

Perceptual hash matching works by comparing the visual fingerprint of your photo against Scryfall's card images. The closer your photo looks to a clean, flat scan, the more accurate the match. Follow these guidelines for consistent results.

Camera Settings

🔭
Use the 1× main lens The 48MP main camera gives the sharpest results at close range. Hold the phone 20–30 cm (8–12 inches) above the card. Avoid the 0.5× ultra-wide lens and never use digital zoom.
🔒
Lock focus and exposure Tap and hold on the card in the viewfinder until AE/AF Lock appears at the top. This prevents the camera from adjusting between shots. Re-lock whenever you move to a new card.
⚡
Turn the flash off The 17 Pro Max flash is powerful enough to blow out the card surface and create a central hotspot that confuses the matcher. Use ambient or natural light instead.
📄
Use ProRAW if storage allows Go to Settings → Camera → Formats and enable Apple ProRAW. This produces a more accurate image with less processing applied. Standard HEIC works well too — just avoid low-quality JPEG settings.

Environment & Lighting

🪟
Diffuse natural light is ideal Place your card near a window on an overcast day — cloud cover acts as a giant softbox and eliminates harsh shadows. Avoid direct sunlight, which creates shadows from the card border and uneven glare.
⬛
Use a plain dark background A dark matte surface — black foam board, a dark desk mat, or a plain dark table — gives the best contrast. Avoid patterned tablecloths, busy surfaces, or other cards nearby.
💡
Position lamps at 45° If using indoor lighting, place two lamps at 45° angles to either side of the card rather than directly above, which creates a circular glare hotspot in the centre.

Positioning the Card

📐
Shoot from directly above Hold the phone parallel to the card surface. Enable the built-in level via Settings → Camera → Level — a yellow crosshair appears and turns white when perfectly parallel. Shoot when it turns white.
🃏
Lay the card completely flat Even slight curl — common with older cards — causes perspective distortion. If a card has curl, press it face-down under a heavy book for a few minutes before photographing.
🔲
Fill 70–80% of the frame The card border should be fully visible with a small margin of background on all four sides. Too close clips the edges; too far loses detail.
📸
Use the Camera Control button The physical Camera Control button on the right side of the phone shoots without nudging the device. With AE/AF locked, simply swap cards and press Camera Control for each shot in a batch.

Troubleshooting

What you see Likely cause Fix
Glare or bright hotspot Flash or overhead light Turn off flash; reposition lamp to 45°
Blurry card Camera refocused mid-batch Re-lock AE/AF by tapping and holding on the card
Card looks trapezoidal Phone not held parallel Use the built-in level; reshoot
Match fails on foil card Holographic surface scatters light Rotate card 45° until glare moves away from the art
Match fails on played card Creases distort the hash Flatten card first; try multiple angles
Dark card (black border) Auto-exposure overexposes After locking AE/AF, swipe down on the ☀️ icon to reduce exposure by 0.5–1 stop

Understanding Match Distance

After a match, the card detail page shows a match distance — a number indicating how visually similar your photo is to Scryfall's reference image. Lower is better; zero is a perfect match.

0 – 7 · Confident match 8 – 12 · Probable match 13 + · Review manually

A note on foil & alternate art cards. Foil, full-art, and borderless treatments look different from the standard printing Scryfall indexes. If a foil card matches with a high distance, the card was likely identified correctly but the visual difference pushed the score up — check the matched card name and use your judgement.
